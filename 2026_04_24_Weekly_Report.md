## 3D Printing / Additive Manufacturing

- **Focused-Microwave Electronics 3D Printing (Rice University)**: A team led by Yong Lin Kong at Rice University demonstrated 3D printing of electronics using focused microwaves, concentrating heating to a zone as small as a human hair's diameter to cure conductive ink without damaging underlying materials — overcoming a decade-old barrier in electronics additive manufacturing. [Source](https://techxplore.com/news/2026-04-3d-electronics-focused-microwaves-redefines.html)

- **Tungsten Carbide–Cobalt 3D Printing**: Scientists achieved 3D printing of one of the world's hardest metals (tungsten carbide–cobalt) using a hot-wire laser technique that softens rather than fully melts the alloy, reaching hardness above 1,400 HV while avoiding defects. [Source](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260313002642.htm)

- **Universal Binder for Multi-Material AM**: Researchers developed a universal binder system enabling multi-material components with contradictory properties — such as ceramic gears with hard outer surfaces and flexible interiors — expanding design freedom in structural AM. [Source](https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1124879)

- **ML-Predicted Mechanical Properties for FDM Polymers**: A Multi-Target Machine Learning framework simultaneously predicts multiple mechanical properties of FDM-printed PLA, ABS, and PETG components from process parameters, reducing the need for expensive physical testing campaigns. [Source](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-49134-6)

- **ORNL 3D-Printed Nuclear Reactor Molds**: Oak Ridge National Laboratory won the 2026 SME award for additively manufactured precision molds for nuclear reactor components, highlighting AM's maturation in safety-critical, high-precision industries. [Source](https://www.voxelmatters.com/ornl-wins-2026-sme-award-for-3d-printed-precision-nuclear-reactor-molds/)

## Computational Sciences

- **Dual Physics-Informed Neural Network for Topology Optimization (DPNN-TO)**: A new method merges PINNs with SIMP-based topology optimization, embedding physical constraints directly into the neural network and eliminating the need for large training datasets or analytical sensitivity analysis. [Source](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0021999126000161)

- **Reinforcement Learning Topology Optimization with PPO**: Researchers applied the Proximal Policy Optimization algorithm to topology optimization, achieving 12×12 grid resolution in 2D problems through smart reward shaping — advancing purely DRL-based structural design without conventional FEA solvers. [Source](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2215016125003838)

- **Newton 1.0 Open-Source Physics Engine**: NVIDIA, co-developed with Google DeepMind and Disney Research under Linux Foundation governance, released Newton 1.0 — a GPU-accelerated, open-source physics engine providing accurate collision detection, realistic object contact, and stable simulation of rigid and flexible systems for robot training. [Source](https://dataconomy.com/2026/03/17/nvidia-launches-newton-1-0-physics-engine-for-industrial-robot-training/)

- **ML-Based Inverse Design for Functional Materials**: A comprehensive review in *Advanced Functional Materials* surveys topology optimization, direct inverse mapping, and hybrid ML frameworks for functional material design, highlighting fast surrogate modeling and efficient design-space exploration as key enablers. [Source](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adfm.75070)

- **AI Geometry Optimization for Lightweight Architectural Structures**: Research combining generative design algorithms, topology optimization, and simulation is enabling architects to computationally explore structurally efficient, materially lightweight forms that connect directly to 3D printing fabrication pipelines. [Source](https://www.fixie3d.com/fixie-blog/2026/4/15/ai-driven-geometry-optimization-the-future-of-lightweight-architectural-prints)

## Soft Robotics & Compliant Mechanisms

- **Bistable Origami Soft Pneumatic Actuators (BOSPA)**: A 2026 study introduced BOSPA — a 3D-printed inflatable Miura-origami tube with two parallel air chambers and elastic rings that achieves snap-through bistability and multiple deformation modes (linear extension, bending, coupled bending/extension), enabling versatile soft-legged locomotion. [Source](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/21695172261424024)

- **Pneumatic Kirigami Actuators for Programmable Motion**: Researchers demonstrated kirigami-based pneumatic actuators that leverage patterned cuts in flat sheets to achieve programmable bending, buckling, and elongation under pressure — enabling versatile robotic functions from a single planar manufacturing process. [Source](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-981-95-2095-4_47)

- **Elastic-Soft Hybrid Pneumatic Actuator with Origami Structure**: New compact hybrid-drive actuator modules combine tendon-driven actuation with a network of inflatable tubes, allowing configurable switching between extension/bending or extension/twisting deformation modes, increasing actuator versatility in a compact form. [Source](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0020740325000219)

- **Meta-Ori: Monolithic Meta-Origami Nonlinear Inflatables**: A novel approach fabricates nonlinear inflatables (Meta-Ori) combining cylindrical mechanical metamaterials with Kresling origami as monolithic FDM-printed parts, enabling complex, tunable nonlinear mechanical behaviors without assembly. [Source](https://arxiv.org/html/2503.23375v1)

- **Fabric-Based Pneumatic Soft Actuators Review**: A comprehensive review published in early 2026 surveys fabric-based pneumatic soft actuator design strategies and emerging applications in flexible robotics, highlighting textile integration as a scalable manufacturing route for wearable and assistive systems. [Source](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12197161/)

## Biology & Medical Research

- **First Personalized In-Body CRISPR Editing**: KJ Muldoon received a bespoke CRISPR therapy targeting a specific mutation causing toxic ammonia accumulation (a rare metabolic disorder), with gene editing performed inside his body — establishing a proof-of-concept for fully personalized, in vivo gene correction at the individual patient level. [Source](https://www.nih.gov/news-events/news-releases/nih-funded-breakthrough-shrinks-crispr-precision-delivery-body)

- **Miniaturized CRISPR Enzyme (Al3Cas12f)**: Researchers identified Al3Cas12f, a naturally occurring enzyme small enough to fit inside adeno-associated virus (AAV) vectors — the leading targeted delivery vehicle for gene therapy — dramatically expanding the range of conditions addressable by in vivo CRISPR without requiring larger delivery vehicles. [Source](https://www.nih.gov/news-events/news-releases/nih-funded-breakthrough-shrinks-crispr-precision-delivery-body)

- **CRISPR Gene Activation Without DNA Cutting**: A new CRISPR technique reactivates silenced genes by removing epigenetic methyl tags rather than cutting DNA, confirming that these chemical anchors actively silence genes and opening a new therapeutic axis for diseases caused by suppressed gene expression. [Source](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/01/260104202813.htm)

- **AI-Driven Drug Target Identification**: A *Nature Reviews Drug Discovery* publication examines how AI is systematically mapping the human genome's ~20,000 protein-coding genes to expand the ~716 currently druggable targets, transforming target identification from serendipity-driven discovery to a data-driven pipeline. [Source](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/nature-reviews-drug-discovery-target-identification-and-assessment-in-the-era-of-ai)

- **Gene Therapies for Inherited Blindness, Sickle Cell & Beta-Thalassemia**: Advances in 2026 have effectively transitioned sickle cell disease and beta-thalassemia from incurable to treatable conditions via gene editing, while therapies for inherited blindness and identification of genetic drivers of ALS and frontotemporal dementia continue to advance the broader gene therapy pipeline. [Source](https://www.livescience.com/health/from-gene-therapy-breakthroughs-to-preventable-disease-outbreaks-the-health-trends-that-will-shape-2026)

## Key Takeaways

- **Physics engines are becoming the connective tissue of robotics**: Newton 1.0's open-source, GPU-accelerated release (NVIDIA + Google DeepMind + Disney Research) creates a shared simulation substrate linking topology optimization, dexterous manipulation training, and sim-to-real transfer — simultaneously advancing Computational Sciences, Robotics, and Soft Robotics.

- **Generative AI has crossed the design-to-fabrication threshold**: From Autodesk Neural CAD generating parametric geometry to TCT Asia 2026's end-to-end digital-to-physical pipelines and NCSA's inverse material design, AI is no longer a design exploration tool but an active participant in production-ready fabrication workflows.

- **CRISPR is fragmenting into specialized precision tools**: Three distinct CRISPR modalities made headlines this week — miniaturized in-body editing (Al3Cas12f + AAV), personalized bespoke in vivo correction (KJ Muldoon), and epigenetic activation without DNA cuts — indicating the field is moving from a single platform toward a toolkit of targeted interventions.

- **Origami principles are unifying soft robotics across scales**: Bistable Miura-origami actuators (BOSPA), kirigami pneumatic actuators, and Meta-Ori monolithic inflatables all appeared in the same week, reflecting convergent momentum toward printable, foldable mechanisms as a design language for compliant systems.

- **Frontier AI model scale and pricing are simultaneously accelerating**: The debut of the first 10-trillion-parameter model (Claude Mythos 5) alongside continued frontier pricing collapse means researchers across all fields now have access to qualitatively more capable AI assistance at lower cost — compressing the time from computational hypothesis to physical experiment.
