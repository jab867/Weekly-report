## Computational Design

- **Autodesk Neural CAD — Text-to-Parametric-Model**: Autodesk's Neural CAD, growing out of Project Bernini, is an AI foundation model that generates fully editable, parametric CAD geometry from a single text prompt. Unlike earlier generative tools that output optimization meshes requiring reinterpretation, Neural CAD delivers models engineers can immediately refine in standard workflows. [Autodesk News](https://adsknews.autodesk.com/en/news/upcoming-3d-generative-ai-foundation-models/)

- **MIT MechStyle — Structurally Sound Generative 3D Design**: MIT researchers introduced MechStyle, a generative AI system that creates personalized 3D objects which are both visually distinctive and mechanically sound by integrating structural analysis directly into the generative design loop — addressing a key gap in AI-generated geometry quality. [Engineering.com](https://www.engineering.com/3-ai-features-coming-to-every-cad-program-in-2026/)

[Autodesk Newsroom](https://adsknews.autodesk.com/en/news/autodesk-flow-studio-launches-wonder-3d-gen-ai-model-to-help-creators-make-3d-characters-and-objects-faster-and-easier/)

## Computational Sciences

- **DRL + Genetic Algorithm Hybrid for Topology Optimization**: New work presents hybrid deep reinforcement learning and genetic algorithm frameworks for structural topology optimization, achieving 12×12 grid resolution via pure DRL and demonstrating up to 40% weight reduction compared to conventional SIMP and level-set methods while maintaining compliance targets. [Science Publishing Group](https://www.sciencepublishinggroup.com/article/10.11648/j.ajasr.20261201.11) | [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2215016125003838)

- **PPO-Based Reinforcement Learning for Lightweight Structures**: An AI-driven generative design framework integrating topology optimization with Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) reinforcement learning outperforms classical methods — generating lightweight, manufacturable mechanical structures with demonstrably better convergence and constraint handling. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2215016125003838)

- **Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) Review for Structural Engineering**: A comprehensive review published in Artificial Intelligence Review covers advances in PINNs applied to structural engineering, cataloguing current capabilities and remaining gaps — particularly the challenge of solving PDEs on arbitrary domains with arbitrary boundary conditions at the scale required for generalized topology optimization. [Springer Nature](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-025-11444-y)

- **Physics-Informed Deep Learning for Topology Optimization Without Gradient Access**: New approaches adopt a deep image prior via CNN to evaluate structural displacement through physics simulation and update network parameters based on a physics-based loss, enabling gradient-free topology optimization for complex simulators such as FEM, CFD, and MBD. [ASTESJ](https://www.astesj.com/v06/i04/p27/)

## Soft Robotics & Compliant Mechanisms

- **Bistable Origami-Inspired Soft Pneumatic Actuator (BOSPA)**: Researchers developed a 3D-printed, inflatable soft Miura-origami tube structure with dual air chambers and strategically placed elastic rings that enable bistability — achieving multiple deformation modes, amplified output force, and rapid motion for soft-legged robotic locomotion. [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/21695172261424024)

- **Modular Soft Origami Pneumatic Actuator (SOPA) with Tunable Stiffness**: A new modular SOPA design achieves bidirectional angular displacement of up to 110° per module with programmable stiffness, enabling soft robotic systems requiring large angular motion and strong load-bearing capability — directly applicable to compliant grippers and wearables. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0020740325008033)

- **Origami-Inspired Pneumatic Elbow Exosuit with EMG Control**: A wearable origami-inspired pneumatic elbow exosuit with EMG-based active rehabilitation control was demonstrated — using a portable air source to drive the actuator and EMG signals from the user's arm to provide adaptive, intention-driven assistance. [MDPI Actuators](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-0825/15/2/127)

- **Advances in Fabric-Based Pneumatic Soft Actuators Review**: A comprehensive PMC review covers recent progress in fabric-based pneumatic soft actuators for flexible robotics — surveying design principles, fabrication strategies, and applications in soft manipulation, wearables, and assistive devices. [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12197161/)

- **Architected Materials for Soft Robotics**: A Journal of Materials Research publication highlights the role of kirigami, origami, auxetic structures, and lattice architectures in soft robotic actuators, sensors, and sensorized systems with embedded sensing capabilities — converging structural design with functional material intelligence. ## Key Takeaways

1. **AI is entering the physical world at scale**: From NASA Mars rovers using Claude to plan drives autonomously, to NVIDIA's RoboLab and Mimic's 10x sample-efficient manipulation models, the gap between AI cognition and physical execution is closing rapidly — with fabrication and robotics workflows among the highest-impact near-term beneficiaries.

2. **Text-to-CAD is becoming production-ready**: Autodesk's Neural CAD (Project Bernini) and MIT's MechStyle both signal that generating structurally valid, editable parametric geometry from text or image inputs is no longer a research demo — it is converging with engineering workflows in SolidWorks 2026, Fusion, and Forma.

3. **Gene therapy is entering a regulatory maturation phase**: The FDA's new "plausible mechanism" framework for N-of-1 therapies, combined with expanded CRISPR trials (Casgevy in children), NEJM publication of ANGPTL3 editing results, and Aurora Therapeutics' launch, collectively signal that personalized gene therapy is shifting from proof-of-concept to scalable clinical reality.

4. **Origami-inspired soft actuators are converging design, fabrication, and function**: Bistable pneumatic origami structures (BOSPA, SOPA), fabric-based actuators, and EMG-controlled exosuits published this week demonstrate that soft robotics is maturing from single-mode demos into multi-functional, manufacturable, wearable systems — with direct relevance to deployable structures and compliant mechanism design.

5. **Topology optimization is being transformed by RL and physics-informed learning**: Hybrid DRL–GA approaches achieving 40% weight reductions over SIMP, combined with gradient-free physics-informed deep learning for FEM/CFD workflows, suggest that AI-driven structural optimization is ready to displace classical methods in fabrication-aware design pipelines.
